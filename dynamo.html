Operation Dynamo

On May 26th, the British started the evacuation of the troops using the codename 'Operation Dynamo.' On the first day, only around 7500 men were evacuated. However, over 10,000 got out the next day. Since Dunkirk had a very shallow beach and Royal Navy vessels were getting bombed on the way back to Dover, the British sent their civilians' boats, many fishing boats, to aid in Dunkirk's evacuation. This armada became known as the "Little Ships," an armada of over 1,000.

Churchill only expected to rescue around 45,000 men at most, but 47,000 were evacuated on the 29th, and over 53,000 vacated on the 30th. After evacuations were finished, a total of 338,000 men were evacuated, and 90,000 were left behind, along with guns and tanks.
